# Behavior Support Plan for Aiden (Needs Revision)

Aiden sometimes gets out of his seat during independent work. This behavior happens a lot and can cause distractions. The main goal of this plan is to help Aiden stay in his seat more often so he can finish his work. Adults should try their best to keep him focused.

---

## Overview

Aiden is an energetic kid who sometimes has trouble sitting still. When independent work starts, he may not want to do it and gets up to walk around. He might be bored or might not understand the work. It is also possible that he is looking for attention or maybe just wants a break.

This plan will outline ways to help him stay seated and try harder during work time.

---

## Strengths

- Friendly  
- Likes science  
- Can be a good worker when he wants to  
- Enjoys positive reinforcement  

---

## Target Behavior

Aiden leaves his seat a lot during class. He wanders around even though he is supposed to be working. This interrupts other students and slows down his progress.

---

## Possible Reasons for the Behavior

Aiden might be:
- Seeking attention  
- Avoiding work  
- Bored  
- Wanting movement breaks  

It is hard to know for sure based on the information provided.

---

## Strategies for Adults

### 1. Tell him to stay in his seat
Remind Aiden before work time that he needs to stay seated. Explain why staying seated is important.

### 2. Give him movement breaks whenever needed
Allow Aiden to get up and move around whenever he seems restless. This can help him burn off energy.

### 3. Provide rewards for staying in his seat
Give Aiden stickers or small prizes if he stays seated for a while.

### 4. Help him right away when he gets up
If he leaves his seat, go over and help him with the work so he does not fall behind.

### 5. Make the work easier
Shorten assignments or reduce expectations so Aiden has less to complete.

---

## Monitoring Progress

Teachers can try to keep track of how often Aiden gets up. If things improve, keep doing what works.

---

## Summary

Aiden struggles with staying in his seat, but by giving him breaks, rewards, and help, adults can make work time easier and help him be more successful.
